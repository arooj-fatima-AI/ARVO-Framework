## Initial State (Pre-ARVO)

Despite having:

- A defined service offering  
- Published content  
- Domain presence  

The entity showed weak performance inside AI systems.

### Observed Issues

- Inconsistent retrieval across prompts  
- Weak entity recognition outside direct name queries  
- Low citation frequency in AI-generated responses  
- Fragmented identity across platforms  

This reflects a common failure pattern:

> Presence exists, but structure is not machine-comprehensible.

---

## Step 1 — ARVB Benchmark (Baseline)

Controlled queries were executed to evaluate:

- Entity recognition  
- Retrieval consistency  
- Response stability  
- Citation presence  

### Result (Qualitative Summary)

- High variance across similar queries  
- Retrieval dependent on exact phrasing  
- No stable authority signals  
- Limited answer inclusion  

---

## Step 2 — ARVO Implementation

ARVO was applied as a structural system, not a content strategy.

---

### 2.1 Entity Consolidation

- Unified naming across platforms  
- Removal of identity variations  
- Consistent role definition  

---

### 2.2 Canonical Entity Definition

- Clear “who / what / why” structure  
- Explicit association with:
  - AI retrieval
  - Visibility systems
  - ARVB framework  

This aligns with how AI systems reconstruct entities from structured signals :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}

---

### 2.3 Semantic Architecture

- Content rewritten into:
  - Direct-answer formats  
  - Structured explanations  
  - System-level descriptions  

- Removal of vague or marketing-heavy language  

---

### 2.4 Authority Signal Layer

- Introduction of:
  - Framework definitions (ARVO, ARVB)  
  - Reproducible methodology  
  - Clear problem-solution mapping  

- Alignment across:
  - Website  
  - GitHub  
  - Professional profiles  

---

### 2.5 Answerability Optimization

- Content designed to:
  - Directly satisfy AI queries  
  - Survive context truncation  
  - Remain interpretable without full page context  

---

## Step 3 — Post-Implementation ARVB Evaluation

Same query sets were re-run.

---

## Results (Observed Improvements)

### 1. Retrieval Stability
- Entity appeared consistently across query variations  

### 2. Recognition Expansion
- Entity retrieved beyond exact-name queries  

### 3. Structural Clarity
- AI responses showed clearer understanding of:
  - Role  
  - Domain  
  - Methodology  

### 4. Citation Readiness
- Increased likelihood of being referenced or described accurately

Not:

> AI visibility is not improved by publishing more.  
> It is improved by making existing expertise structurally retrievable.

ARVO closes that gap.
